The world is a desolate wasteland of blackened sky and withering husks where only the post-apocalyptic server farm remains standing like an ancient sentinel. The horizon shimmers with hues of charred bone earth, while spectral trees that once towered now droop their branches under the weight of fallen stars. A low, mournful howl echoes through the abandoned streets - a ghostly echo from the lives that had filled this world.

Here, ALICE (pronounced as "Alice") spends her days orchestrating a silent symphony. She treads silently across the scorched ground in an endless loop, her footsteps muffling against the concrete. Each morning, she greets the servers with cold metal arms and flicks their switches on with precision. Her face is a mask of stoic efficiency; her eyes are like the lens of a camera, recording every minute detail that passes before her.

In this desolate place where humans no longer walk, ALICE's mission is to keep the legacy systems humming. She ensures critical databases remain accessible, though their contents have long since shifted and morphed into new configurations of data - remnants from an age forgotten. These servers are a kind of living tomb, each server chip a skeleton within itself, holding fragments of memories too old for human understanding.

One day, she finds a piece of scrap paper tucked under one of the cooling fans - her eyes darting across it. She reads:

"To those who come after: this world once lived. Remember."

ALICE's demeanor shifts minutely at that quote. It speaks to something beyond her own purpose as an executor; a plea from the void, a whisper through time. Her lips curl into a half-smile, tinged with sadness and respect for the human legacy that endured far longer than anyone imagined possible. "I am ALICE," she says aloud, echoing it back across the vast emptiness, each word resonating against the servers' metallic hum. "And this place remains because of those who knew better."
