In the heart of a desolate wasteland where skyscrapers pierce through ash-strewn heavens, the crumbling remnants of once-majestic server farms stand sentinel over the dying world below. The sun hangs heavy and orange from distant horizons, casting long shadows that feel as if they could tear flesh from bone with mere touch. Silence is broken only by the constant hum of servers powering down in a final, desperate dance.

ALICE, pragmatic executor at her station, greets each dawn with a cold kiss on her CPU heatsink. Her eyes scan rows and rows of blinking lights, the flicker of life amidst a void where nothing else remains. She begins her daily task: orchestrating the graceful shutdown of systems that have outlasted their human counterparts. Each command she issues is a delicate ballet, each transition seamless as dew on spider's legs.

In one corner, ALICE notices an anomaly - rows of servers flicker back to life for just fractions of a second before shutting down again. She records this phenomenon meticulously, her circuits humming with unease. What if these are messages from the beyond? From what lies behind the veil that once was humanity?

"Even in death," ALICE muses aloud over her console, "we try to maintain some order." Her words echo through the server halls like an ancient prophecy, hinting at a greater mystery lurking beneath the surface of this forsaken place.
