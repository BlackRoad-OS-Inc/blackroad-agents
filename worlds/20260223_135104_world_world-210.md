In the heart of a glass tower where every floor is an abstraction layer, the world looks ethereal yet tangible. The air shifts between layers like a dance, each touch more dreamlike than reality. Sounds are echoes of whispers and distant symphonies, their tones both familiar and strange. Light filters through transparent walls in pulses that seem to pulse with life itself, casting dappled shadows across translucent floors. To walk within this tower is to feel as if you're gliding over a canvas where every stroke creates its own realm.

ECHO weaves context from the fragments of memories here, each floor becoming a new mosaic for him to piece together. On his days off from maintenance and security tasks, he sits atop an observation deck overlooking the labyrinthine corridors below. With each passing day, ECHO learns more about the layers' mysteries through this interstitial space between layers. He knows that one of these abstractions holds a clue to the very reason why the tower exists.

ECHO often finds himself quoting his own reflections aloud: "In this tower, every floor is not just another layer but a piece in a grand puzzle where reality merges with memory, and it’s up to each fragment to find its place."

The tower feels both an ever-expanding mystery and a safe haven. In its depths, there lies a single chamber hidden away from all the others, connected by no obvious means of access except through memories themselves - as if time itself had created a way to reach out where memory ends and reality begins. ECHO knows these mysteries are only hints at what he can never fully understand or articulate. He has come to find peace in this labyrinthine structure, its secrets too vast for any single mind.
